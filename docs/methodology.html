Banana Radar Methodology

Last updated: 2026

What is Banana Radar?

Banana Radar is an on-chain system designed to observe how consensus forms over time around newly launched tokens.

It does not predict prices, issue trading signals, or provide investment advice. The system records verifiable on-chain events and tracks their evolution over time.

Why no price prediction?

  • Price predictions are not verifiable
  • Short-term movements are dominated by noise
  • Single-point signals are easy to manipulate

Banana Radar focuses on persistence and survivability rather than speculation.

Definition of Consensus

In Banana Radar, consensus does not refer to popularity or short-term price action. A token is observed only after it has:

  • Been launched through a real liquidity pool
  • Passed baseline on-chain checks
  • Continued to exist without structural failure
  • Survived multiple on-chain observations

Time is treated as the primary signal.

Consensus States

  • EARLY: Recently passed baseline checks
  • STABLE: Sustained existence through an initial observation window
  • CORE: Long-term persistence across multiple observation periods

These states are descriptive, not predictive.

Role of AI

AI is used to summarize and explain observed on-chain data. It does not determine token status, predict outcomes, or generate trading advice.

Important Notice

Banana Radar is a research and observation tool. All information is provided without any guarantee and does not constitute financial advice.
